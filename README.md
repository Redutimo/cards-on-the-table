# AI — Cards on the Table

**A persona-based conversation exercise for the Moving Targets Workshop · BLE**

Live: [https://redutimo.github.io/cards-on-the-table/](https://redutimo.github.io/cards-on-the-table/)

---

## What is this?

*Cards on the Table* is a facilitated workshop activity that uses fictional personas to surface honest conversations about AI in higher education. Participants draw from one of two decks — **HE Staff** or **Students** — and use the characters' stories, anxieties, and shadow thoughts as a starting point for discussion.

There are no right answers here. Only honest ones.

---

## How it works

1. Open the link above in a browser (no install required)
2. Choose a deck — **HE Staff** or **Students**
3. Click any card face-down to flip it and reveal the persona
4. Open the card to read their quote, shadow thought, and anxiety tags
5. Use the **Discussion Prompts** to open a conversation
6. The **Facilitator Note** at the bottom of each card offers guidance on what the character is really getting at

Cards can be reset and replayed. The tool works on any device.

---

## The decks

### HE Staff
Personas from across higher education — academic, library, digital, and professional support roles. Characters grapple with questions of authenticity, workload, trust, and professional identity in the age of AI.

### Students
Personas navigating AI in their studies — the pressures, the shortcuts, the grey areas, and the things they haven't quite admitted yet.

## Facilitator guidance

This activity works best when:
- Participants choose a card that resonates with them (or that unsettles them)
- The facilitator holds space for discomfort — the shadow thoughts are intentionally provocative
- Discussion stays with the *character's* perspective before moving to personal experience
- There is no pressure to reach consensus

Estimated time: 20–45 minutes depending on group size and depth of discussion.
